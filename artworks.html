Famous Artworks

The Two Fridas (Las dos Fridas, 1939)

Frida Kahlo's most famous oil painting depicts two versions of herself sitting side by side, holding hands. The painting is often interpreted as a representation of Kahlo's dual heritage and her struggles with identity and self-acceptance. The two Fridas are connected by a vein that runs between their hearts, symbolizing their shared pain and emotional connection. Frida Kahlo's use of vibrant colors and surreal imagery in this painting has made it an iconic piece of art that continues to resonate and inspire those in the art world.

Walk on the beach, 1901

Joaquín Sorolla's most famous oil painting shows two women, Sorolla's wife Clotilde and eldest daughter María. Walking along the shoreline of the beach, with the sunlight reflecting off the water and sand. The painting is often interpreted as a celebration of the beauty of nature and the joy of life. Sorolla's use of light and color in this painting has made it an iconic piece of art that continues to impress audiences around the world.

The Persistence of Memory, 1931

Salvador Dalí's most famous oil painting, features melting clocks draped over a barren landscape. The painting is often interpreted as a representation of the fluidity of time and the subconscious mind. Dali's use of surreal imagery and dreamlike landscapes in this painting has made it an iconic piece of art that continues to captivate audiences globally.

The Farm, 1921

Joan Miró's most famous oil painting, depicts a rural landscape with a farmhouse, trees, and animals. The painting is often interpreted as a celebration of the beauty of nature and the simplicity of rural life. Miró's use of bright colors and whimsical shapes in this painting has made it an iconic piece of art that continues to inspire audiences around the world.

The People for the University. The University for the People 1952

David Alfaro Siqueiros' most famous mural painting, depicts the history of humanity in Latin America, from pre-Columbian times to the present day. This mural is typically viewed as a representation of the resilience and strength of the Latin American people. Siqueiros' incorporates bold colors and dynamic compositions in this mural, transforming it into an iconic artwork that resonates with and influences the Latin American community to this very day.

Guernica, 1937

Pablo Picasso's most famous oil painting, displays the bombing of the town of Guernica during the Spanish Civil War. The painting is often interpreted as a powerful anti-war statement and a representation of the horrors of war. Picasso's use of monochromatic colors and distorted figures in this painting has made it an iconic piece of art that continues to resonate with audiences around the world.
